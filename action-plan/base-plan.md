Here's the full plan for **Store Kit for WooCommerce** — a module map where you can toggle features on/off. Here's the strategic thinking behind it all.

---

## Why a modular all-in-one is the right move

The WooCommerce plugin market is crowded at the individual plugin level. The gap is in the **bundled, coherent experience** — store owners don't want to vet 12 separate plugins, manage 12 update cycles, and debug 12 compatibility issues. Store Kit wins by being the one plugin they actually trust to run their store.

The toggle-per-module architecture is critical because it keeps the plugin lightweight (your core value at JetixWP) while covering a wide surface area. A store running only the Core modules loads almost nothing extra.

---

## Module tiers explained

**Core modules** (the must-haves that justify installation on day one):
These are the features virtually every WooCommerce store eventually needs — Quick View, Cart Notices, Checkout Field Editor, Custom Order Statuses, Stock Manager, and Product Tab Manager. They solve real daily friction. Lead with these in your marketing because they're easy to justify to a store owner in one sentence.

**Growth modules** (conversion and engagement):
Wishlist, Free Shipping Bar, Variation Swatches, Sale Badges, Ajax Search, and Recently Viewed. These are the features store owners see on competitor sites and want. They move the revenue needle directly and are easy to demo.

**Power modules** (for mature stores and upsell):
Points & Rewards, Bulk Pricing, Back-in-Stock Waitlist, Advanced Reviews, and Order Export. These are stickier, harder to rip out once installed, and justify a premium tier or annual renewal. They also tend to be the features that competing standalone plugins charge $49–$99/year for individually.

How Store Kit helps you reach 20% market share
The math here is about footprint, not features. Each store that installs Store Kit and enables even three modules becomes a store that no longer needs three other plugins. That's how you displace established players — not by being cheaper, but by being already there and trusted.
A few specific levers:
Distribution: Submit to the free WordPress.org repository with a Lite version (Core modules only, no power features). The plugin directory is still one of the strongest organic growth channels in the WordPress ecosystem. Pair it with a Pro upgrade path unlocking Growth and Power modules.
Compatibility-first positioning: Make a public compatibility matrix. Document that Store Kit works cleanly with Elementor, Divi, Astra, Kadence, and the major WooCommerce extensions (Subscriptions, Bookings, etc.). Compatibility anxiety is a top reason store owners avoid switching plugins — remove that anxiety.
Marketing channels that actually work for WooCommerce plugins: YouTube tutorials targeting specific use-case queries ("how to add a wishlist to WooCommerce", "WooCommerce free shipping bar"), the MakeWebBetter/WPBeginner/Barn2 content ecosystem, and Reddit communities like r/woocommerce and r/WordPress. Facebook Groups for WooCommerce store owners are still surprisingly active and high-intent.
Pricing angle: Bundle Store Kit Pro at a price that undercuts buying 4–5 individual plugins separately. The value story writes itself — "everything you'd buy anyway, one license, one update."
